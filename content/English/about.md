## Research Overview

> **Renormalized information geometry at singular terminals sits at the intersection of three distinct traditions.** Classical information geometry studies Fisher metrics on smooth statistical manifolds, typically strictly confined within a fixed absolute-continuity class (the "interior metric"). Optimal transport supplies the metric geometry of probability measures and governs entropy gradient flows (the "kinematics"). Finite-part renormalization extracts canonical quantities from singular asymptotic expansions (the "survival of the finite through the wall"). 
>
> **The present program unifies these viewpoints at the boundary.** When a family of models approaches a singular terminal and fundamentally breaks the equivalent-measure regime, ordinary Fisher, entropy, and transport quantities inherently diverge or collapse. Yet, their finite-part residues survive to define a completely new boundary geometry. 
> 
> This framework manifests uniformly across different domains: in Gaussian conditioning, this residue strictly yields the **Cameron--Martin energy**; in Wasserstein terminal limits, it emerges as a **finite-part entropy slope**; and in Markov information channels, it crystallizes into an **infinitesimal Shannon operator**.
